Porridge with Banana & Cinnamon
V1
~$0.60
The ultimate budget breakfast — filling, fibre-rich oats sweetened naturally with banana.
~330 kcal
10g protein
~80mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • ½ cup rolled oats (~$0.20)
  • 1 cup water or low-fat milk (~$0.15)
  • 1 banana, sliced (~$0.20)
  • ½ tsp cinnamon (~$0.02)
  • 1 tsp honey or a few raisins (optional)

Method

  • Combine oats and liquid in a saucepan
  • Cook over medium heat, stirring, 4–5 min
  • Pour into a bowl, top with sliced banana
  • Dust with cinnamon and serve
Tip: Buy oats in bulk — a 1kg bag lasts weeks and costs very little per serve. Use water instead of milk to cut cost further without losing much nutrition.
Budget Win: Rolled oats are one of the most nutritious foods per dollar available. Avoid flavoured instant oat packets — they cost 3× more and are full of sugar and sodium.
Scrambled Eggs on Wholegrain Toast
V2
~$1.10
High-protein, fast, and satisfying — eggs are the best value protein source available.
~350 kcal
22g protein
~220mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs (~$0.75)
  • 1 slice low-sodium wholegrain bread (~$0.20)
  • 1 tsp olive oil or butter (~$0.05)
  • Black pepper, garlic powder, fresh parsley
  • Optional: ½ tomato, sliced alongside

Method

  • Whisk eggs with pepper and garlic powder
  • Cook slowly in a pan on low heat, folding gently
  • Remove just before fully set — residual heat finishes them
  • Serve on toast with fresh parsley and tomato
Tip: Low and slow is the key to creamy scrambled eggs. High heat gives you rubbery eggs. Season with herbs and pepper — no salt needed.
Budget Win: A dozen eggs costs very little and provides 12 high-quality protein servings. Buy the largest carton available for the best per-egg price.
Overnight Oats with Frozen Berries
V3
~$0.90
Zero morning effort — prep takes 2 minutes the night before. Frozen berries cost a fraction of fresh.
~340 kcal
12g protein
~95mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • ½ cup rolled oats (~$0.20)
  • ¾ cup low-fat milk or water (~$0.15)
  • ⅓ cup frozen mixed berries (~$0.40)
  • 1 tsp chia seeds (optional, ~$0.10)
  • ½ tsp cinnamon, pinch of nutmeg

Method

  • Mix oats, milk, cinnamon, and chia in a jar or bowl
  • Add frozen berries on top (they thaw overnight)
  • Cover and refrigerate overnight
  • Stir in the morning and eat cold or microwave 1 min
Tip: Frozen berries are just as nutritious as fresh — often more so, since they're frozen at peak ripeness. They cost 3–4× less than fresh.
Budget Win: Make 3–4 jars at once on Sunday. Stack them in the fridge for grab-and-go breakfasts all week with zero extra effort.
Banana Oat Pancakes (2-Ingredient)
V4
~$0.80
Just banana and egg — naturally sweet, gluten-free pancakes with zero added sugar or salt.
~310 kcal
14g protein
~110mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe banana (~$0.20)
  • 2 eggs (~$0.50)
  • 2 tbsp rolled oats (~$0.05, optional for texture)
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • Cooking spray or tiny drop of oil

Method

  • Mash banana thoroughly in a bowl
  • Whisk in eggs and cinnamon (and oats if using)
  • Cook small rounds in a lightly oiled pan on medium-low
  • Flip when bubbles form and edges look set (~2 min each side)
  • Top with more banana or a drizzle of honey
Tip: Use the ripest, most spotted banana you have — the riper it is, the sweeter and easier to mash. These don't flip like regular pancakes; be patient and use a thin spatula.
Budget Win: This whole meal costs under a dollar. It's a great way to use bananas that are too ripe to eat on their own.

Lentil & Vegetable Soup
V1
~$1.20
A hearty, protein-packed soup that batch-cooks beautifully — costs cents per bowl.
~380 kcal
20g protein
~220mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • ½ cup dried red lentils (~$0.30)
  • 1 carrot, 1 celery stalk, ½ onion (~$0.30)
  • 1 cup no-added-salt canned tomatoes (~$0.40)
  • 2 cups water or homemade stock (~$0.05)
  • 1 tsp cumin, ½ tsp turmeric, black pepper
  • 1 tsp olive oil

Method

  • Sauté onion, carrot, celery in oil until soft
  • Add spices and cook 1 min
  • Add lentils, tomatoes, and water
  • Simmer 20–25 min until lentils are soft
  • Blend partially or serve chunky with a squeeze of lemon
Tip: Use water instead of stock to control sodium completely. Boost flavour with extra spices — cumin, turmeric, and smoked paprika are powerful and cheap.
Budget Win: A 500g bag of dried lentils costs very little and makes 8–10 servings. This is one of the cheapest and most nutritious meals you can make.
Egg & Vegetable Fried Rice
V2
~$1.40
Use leftover rice and frozen veg for a fast, filling lunch that comes together in 10 minutes.
~420 kcal
18g protein
~310mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup cooked brown rice (~$0.20)
  • 2 eggs (~$0.50)
  • ½ cup frozen mixed veg (peas, corn, carrot) (~$0.30)
  • 1 garlic clove, ½ tsp ginger
  • 1 tsp low-sodium soy sauce or tamari (~$0.10)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil or vegetable oil (~$0.05)

Method

  • Heat oil in a pan or wok over high heat
  • Add garlic and ginger, stir 30 seconds
  • Add frozen veg and cook until thawed
  • Push to the side, scramble eggs in the gap
  • Mix everything together with rice and soy sauce
Tip: Cold leftover rice fries much better than freshly cooked rice — it's drier and the grains stay separate. Cook a big batch of rice on Sunday.
Budget Win: Frozen mixed vegetables cost a fraction of fresh and have near-identical nutrition. A 1kg bag lasts all week and forms the base of multiple meals.
Chickpea & Spinach Stew
V3
~$1.30
A thick, warming stew bursting with plant protein, iron, and bold spice — on the table in 20 minutes.
~400 kcal
18g protein
~230mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • 1 can chickpeas, rinsed (~$0.60)
  • 1 cup frozen or fresh spinach (~$0.30)
  • ½ can no-added-salt diced tomatoes (~$0.30)
  • ½ onion, 2 garlic cloves (~$0.10)
  • 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp smoked paprika, chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp olive oil, squeeze of lemon

Method

  • Sauté onion and garlic in oil until golden
  • Add spices and stir 1 min
  • Add chickpeas and tomatoes, simmer 10 min
  • Stir in spinach until wilted
  • Finish with lemon juice, serve with bread or rice
Tip: Always rinse canned chickpeas under cold water — this washes away up to 40% of the sodium from the brine. It takes 10 seconds and makes a real difference.
Budget Win: Canned chickpeas are one of the best value protein sources. Pair with the cheapest bread you can find (check it's low-sodium) to round out the meal.

  • ½ cucumber, sliced (~$0.30)
  • ½ tomato or a handful of cherry tomatoes (~$0.20)

Ingredients

Budget Win: Canned beans are nutritionally comparable to dried beans but require no soaking or cooking time. Rinse them well and they become a near-zero-sodium protein source.

~500 kcal

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup cooked brown rice (~$0.20)

Red Lentil Dahl with Rice
V2
~$1.40
A deeply satisfying, fully plant-based dinner — fragrant, spiced, and extremely cheap to make.
~470 kcal
22g protein
~180mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • ½ cup red lentils (~$0.30)
  • ¾ cup cooked brown rice (~$0.20)
  • ½ onion, 2 garlic cloves, 1 tsp fresh ginger (~$0.15)
  • ½ cup no-added-salt diced tomatoes (~$0.25)
  • 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp garam masala (~$0.10)
  • 1 tsp olive oil, lemon juice, fresh coriander

Method

  • Sauté onion, garlic, ginger in oil until golden
  • Add spices and stir 1–2 min
  • Add rinsed lentils, tomatoes, and 1.5 cups water
  • Simmer 20 min until lentils are thick and soft
  • Finish with lemon juice and coriander, serve over rice
Tip: The key to great dahl is toasting your spices in oil before adding liquid — this "blooms" the flavours and makes the dish taste restaurant-quality with no salt needed.
Budget Win: This meal costs under $1.50 for a generous, filling serve. Make double and refrigerate — dahl improves overnight as the spices develop further.
Baked Potato with Cottage Cheese & Salad

~450 kcal
24g protein
~290mg sodium

Ingredients

  • 1 large potato (~$0.40)
  • ½ cup low-fat cottage cheese (~$0.60)
  • Mixed salad leaves (~$0.30)
  • Tomato, cucumber (~$0.20)
  • Black pepper, chives or spring onion, lemon juice

Method

  • Scrub potato, prick all over with a fork
  • Bake at 200°C for 50–60 min until soft inside, or microwave 8–10 min

Budget Win: Potatoes are one of the most underrated budget foods — high in potassium (great for blood pressure), filling, and versatile. Buy a large bag for maximum savings.

V4
~$1.80
A one-pan dinner using eggs and whatever veg you have — delicious hot or cold, great for leftovers.
~420 kcal
28g protein
~320mg sodium
Tap for ingredients & method →

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs (~$1.00)
  • ½ cup frozen peas or corn (~$0.20)
  • ½ onion, ½ capsicum, 1 small potato (pre-cooked) (~$0.30)
  • Handful of spinach (~$0.20)

Budget & Health Tips

Eat well for less, every day
💰 The Budget Staples List
Build every shop around these: eggs, rolled oats, brown rice, dried lentils, canned chickpeas/beans (no added salt), plain yogurt (large tub), frozen mixed vegetables, frozen berries, bananas, carrots, potatoes, canned tomatoes (no added salt), peanut butter (no added salt), olive oil. These 15 items cover almost every meal in this plan and cost very little per serve.
🧂 Flavour Without Salt
The cheapest flavour boosters are also the healthiest: garlic, onion, ginger, lemon juice, cumin, turmeric, smoked paprika, cinnamon, black pepper, chilli flakes, dried herbs (oregano, thyme, mixed herbs). Buy these in large bags from the bulk or international food aisle — they cost a fraction of branded spice jars.
📅 Sunday Batch Prep
Spend 60 minutes on Sunday: cook a big pot of brown rice, boil 6 eggs, make a batch of lentil soup or dahl, prep overnight oats for 3 days, and make a batch of oat energy balls. This gives you breakfasts, lunches, dinners, and snacks ready to go all week — and removes the temptation to buy expensive, salty convenience food when you're tired.
🛒 Smart Shopping Rules
Always buy: frozen over fresh for berries and mixed veg (same nutrition, 3–4× cheaper), large tubs over individual portions of yogurt, bulk bags over small packets for oats/rice/lentils, seasonal produce (it's cheaper and fresher). Always check sodium labels: aim for <120mg per 100g. The words "no added salt" on canned goods are your best friends.
⚖️ Daily Cost & Calorie Summary
This plan averages ~$5–7 per day for all meals (breakfast + lunch + dinner + 1–2 snacks) and ~1,400–1,600 kcal. Sodium stays well under 1,200mg/day — below the 1,500mg recommended limit for blood pressure management. For weight loss, aim for a 300–500 kcal deficit from your maintenance level. Drink 2–3 litres of water daily.
